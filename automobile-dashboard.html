Automobile Store Dashboard

How is Store 112 performing and who is driving results?

← Back to Portfolio

This project was built around a straightforward but important business question: how is Store 112 doing? Rather than just pulling numbers, the goal was to build a dashboard that gave store management a clear, at-a-glance picture of sales performance, staff contributions, and what was actually moving off the lot.

The Goal

Store managers need to make decisions quickly. Who is the top salesperson this month? Which car models are selling and which are sitting? How does this month compare to last month? This dashboard was designed to answer those questions without anyone having to dig through spreadsheets.

Tools used: Tableau, Data Visualization, Sales Analysis

What the Dashboard Shows

  • Top salespeople — ranked by units sold and revenue generated, giving management a clear view of who is performing and who may need support
  • Cars sold by model — breaking down which vehicles are most popular and contributing most to revenue
  • Sales trends over time — showing how the store is tracking month over month through 2021
  • Repairs and projects — tracking service department activity alongside sales to give a fuller picture of store operations
  • Employee performance — combining sales and service data to show overall staff contributions across the store

What I Learned

Building this dashboard pushed me to think carefully about how to present information for a non-technical audience. A store manager does not want to see raw data tables. They want to know at a glance whether things are going well or not, and where to focus their attention. That shift in thinking from analysis to communication is something I carry into every project now.

This was also one of my earlier Tableau projects so it helped me get comfortable with calculated fields, filters, and building layouts that are actually useful rather than just visually busy.
